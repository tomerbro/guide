Agentic Engineering

GUIDE

Five principles for engineers building software with AI agents – a framework for staying in control while moving fast.

Think SOLID for object-oriented design, but for the age of AI-assisted engineering.

G

🛡️ Guardrails first

Define constraints, tests, and boundaries before generating code. Establish what "good" looks like up front so AI outputs can be validated automatically. Without guardrails, speed becomes a liability.

U

🧠 Understand the system

AI can write code, but engineers must understand architecture and domain context. Delegating generation without comprehension creates fragile systems no one can maintain or debug.

I

🔄 Iterate with feedback

Treat AI outputs as drafts – review, test, and refine continuously. The first result is rarely the best. Build tight feedback loops between human judgment and AI generation.

D

📐 Design before generation

Architecture and interfaces should be defined by humans. Let engineers own the "what" and "why" while AI assists with the "how." Structure precedes speed.

E

📊 Evaluate everything

Measure quality, correctness, and impact of AI-generated work. What isn't measured can't be improved. Continuous evaluation keeps AI contributions aligned with real-world outcomes.

Engineering with AI, not after it

GUIDE isn't about slowing down – it's about staying in control. These principles help teams harness AI agents effectively while preserving the craftsmanship that makes software reliable.
